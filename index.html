About Me

GIS Analyst at the World Health Organization

I am a Survey Engineer specializing in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and Machine Learning, currently serving as a GIS Analyst at the WHO Regional Office for Africa in Brazzaville. My work focuses on leveraging geospatial technologies to support public health interventions across the African continent.

At the WHO AFRO GIS Centre, I have played a key role in polio eradication efforts, developing innovative mapping solutions that enable real-time monitoring of vaccination campaigns. My work with the GIS Centre has contributed to Africa being certified wild-type poliovirus-free in 2020.

I believe that "a boxplot is to attribute data what a map is to spatial data" - both are powerful tools for uncovering patterns and outliers that drive informed decision-making.
